THE GOBLET OF FIRE

n Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, 14-year-old Harry is mysteriously selected as a fourth contestant in the dangerous Triwizard Tournament. He must survive three perilous tasks: battling a dragon, rescuing friends from the Black Lake, and navigating a lethal magical maze. Along the way, he faces teenage social pressures at the Yule Ball and a growing mystery surrounding his entry into the contest. In the final task, the Triwizard Cup transports Harry and Cedric Diggory to a graveyard where Lord Voldemort is resurrected using Harry’s blood. After a narrow escape that leaves Cedric dead, Harry returns to Hogwarts to warn the wizarding world of Voldemort's return. It is finally revealed that an impostor, Barty Crouch Jr., had manipulated the tournament from within the school to deliver Harry to the Dark Lord.


HARRY POTTER AND THE ORDER OF PHOENIX

In Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix, the Ministry of Magic denies Voldemort’s return and installs the cruel Dolores Umbridge at Hogwarts. Since she refuses to teach real magic, Harry starts a secret club called Dumbledore’s Army to train his friends for battle. Harry is eventually lured to the Ministry of Magic by a false vision, where he and his friends are attacked by Death Eaters. The Order of the Phoenix arrives to help, but during the fight, Harry’s godfather Sirius Black is tragically killed. Voldemort is finally seen by the public, forcing the government to admit that Harry was telling the truth all along. Dumbledore then reveals a secret prophecy: Harry is the "Chosen One" who must eventually defeat Voldemort or be killed by him.


HARRY POTTER AND THE HALF BLOOD PRINCE

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (Book/Movie 6) follows Harry's sixth year at Hogwarts as Voldemort's power grows, focusing on his private lessons with Dumbledore to uncover the Dark Lord's past and secrets to immortality, primarily through Horcruxes, while he excels in Potions using a mysterious book from the "Half-Blood Prince" and navigates teenage romance, culminating in a tragic death at Hogwarts.The story ends with a devastating event on the Astronomy Tower where, after Draco lures Dumbledore into a trap, Severus Snape, revealing himself as the Half-Blood Prince, kills Dumbledore as planned by Dumbledore himself to spare Draco, leaving Harry heartbroken and determined for revenge.


HARRY POTTER DEATHLY HALOOWS

PART 1

Thinking In Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1, Harry, Ron, and Hermione abandon their final year at Hogwarts to embark on a perilous mission to find and destroy Voldemort’s remaining Horcruxes. As the Ministry of Magic falls under the Dark Lord's control, the trio is forced into a life of isolation, moving between hidden campsites to evade capture while struggling with the corrupting influence of a cursed locket. Their journey leads them to discover the legend of the Deathly Hallows—three ancient objects that supposedly make their master the conqueror of Death—just as internal tensions cause Ron to briefly desert the group. After a narrow escape from an ambush at Godric's Hollow and a harrowing confrontation at Malfoy Manor, the group is rescued by Dobby the house-elf, who tragically loses his life in the process. The film concludes on a somber note with Harry burying his friend, while Voldemort desecrates Dumbledore’s grave to seize the Elder Wand, setting the stage for the final battle.

PART 2

in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2, the journey concludes as Harry, Ron, and Hermione break into Gringotts Bank and eventually return to Hogwarts to find and destroy the final Horcruxes. Their return triggers an all-out war at the school, where beloved friends fall and the dark secrets of Severus Snape’s lifelong loyalty to Lily Potter are finally revealed through his memories. Harry discovers that he himself is a Horcrux and must sacrifice his life to make Voldemort mortal, leading to a surreal encounter with Dumbledore in limbo before his miraculous return. The climax sees Neville Longbottom destroy the final Horcrux, Nagini, allowing Harry to defeat Voldemort once and for all as the Elder Wand’s true master. After the battle, Harry rejects the wand's power, and an epilogue nineteen years later shows the trio's children heading off to Hogwarts, signaling that peace has finally been restored.
